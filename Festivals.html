Ati-Atihan Festival

While the main celebration of Ati-Atihan is in Kalibo, Aklan's capital, the festival's influence can be felt in Malay and Boracay as well. This festival typically occurs in January and involves vibrant street dances, colorful costumes, and lively music, paying homage to the Santo Ni√±o (Infant Jesus).

Saguibin Festival

The Saguibin Festival in Malay, Aklan, Philippines, celebrates St. Vincent Ferrer, the patron saint of fishermen, with colorful street parades, cultural performances, and competitions. Honoring the town's fishing heritage, it brings residents and visitors together to celebrate community spirit and cultural pride.

Tinuom Festival

The Tinuom Festival in Malay, Aklan, Philippines, celebrates the municipality's cultural heritage and culinary traditions, featuring the local dish "tinuom," a flavorful chicken soup cooked in bamboo tubes. The festival offers food tastings, cooking demos, and cultural performances, promoting local cuisine and tourism while preserving community traditions.

Boracay International Dragon Boat Festival

This event usually takes place in April or May and attracts dragon boat teams from around the world. Races are held along Boracay's coastline, providing thrilling competitions and cultural exchanges.

Pascua sa Malinao Festival

Pascua sa Malinao Festival" is an annual Christmas celebration held in the barangay of Malinao, Malay, Aklan. It features parades, street decorations, cultural performances, Christmas bazaars, and community activities, bringing together residents and visitors to celebrate the holiday season with joy and festivity.

Fiesta De Obreros

Fiesta de Obreros in Malay, Aklan, pays tribute to the municipality's workers and its Patron Saint, St. Joseph the Worker. The event features street dancing, PASADA presentations, and merry-making, celebrating the community's prosperity and unity since its inception in 2003.
